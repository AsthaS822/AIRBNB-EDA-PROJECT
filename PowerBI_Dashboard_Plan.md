# Airbnb Data Analytics Project - Power BI Guide

This document outlines the exact steps to build your Power BI dashboard based on the `clean_airbnb.csv` dataset generated by our Python script, along with business insights and a resume bullet point.

---

## Power BI Instructions

**Step 1: Load Data**
1. Open Power BI Desktop.
2. Click **Get Data > Text/CSV**, and load the `Airbnb Data/clean_airbnb.csv` file you just generated.
3. Click **Load**. (If dates or numbers are formatted incorrectly, click **Transform Data** to fix their types, but they should be correct).

### Page 1: Executive Overview (The Big Picture)
*Keep this page simple and clean. It's meant to give stakeholders a 5-second summary.*

- **KPI Cards (Top of page):**
  - **Average Price:** Use the `price` field, set aggregation to `Average`. Format as Currency.
  - **Total Listings:** Use the `price` field, set aggregation to `Count`.
  - **Average Rating:** Use the `review_scores_rating` field, set aggregation to `Average`.
- **Donut Chart:** 
  - **Legend:** `price_segment`
  - **Values:** `Count of listing_id` (Shows the market split of Budget, Mid-range, Premium, vs. Luxury).
- **Clustered Bar Chart:** 
  - **X-axis:** `room_type`
  - **Y-axis:** `Average of price` (Shows which types of properties generate the most revenue).

### Page 2: Market & Location Analysis
*This page focuses on geography and finding the best value.*

- **Map Visual:** 
  - **Location:** `city`
  - **Bubble Size:** `Count of listing_id` (Shows where listings are concentrated).
- **Stacked Column Chart:** 
  - **X-axis:** `city`
  - **Legend:** `price_evaluation` (Underpriced/Fair/Overpriced)
  - **Y-axis:** `Count of listing_id` (Shows which cities have the most overpriced or underpriced listings).
- **Table:** 
  - **Columns:** `city`, `Average of price`, `Average of value_score`. 
  - *Tip: Sort by `value_score` descending to easily spot the best-value cities.*

### Page 3: Seasonality & Demand Trends
*This page shows how the market changes over time.*

- **Line Chart (Pricing Trends):** 
  - **X-axis:** `month`
  - **Y-axis:** `Average of price`
- **Line Chart (Availability Trends):** 
  - **X-axis:** `month`
  - **Y-axis:** `Average of availability_365`
- **Slicers (Filters on the left side):** 
  - Add a slicer for `city` and a slicer for `room_type`. This allows users to filter the line charts interactively based on what they care about.

## Business Insights

*These are 5–7 simple, human-like insights you can talk about during a presentation or interview based on this project structure:*

1. **The "Entire Home" Premium:** Entire homes and apartments command significantly higher average prices compared to private rooms, which suggests travelers are willing to pay a premium for privacy and exclusive amenities over shared spaces.
2. **Value City Opportunities:** While major tourist hubs naturally have the highest average prices, some secondary cities actually score higher on our `value_score` metric. These cities represent great opportunities for budget-conscious travelers looking for high-rated stays.
3. **Market Saturation:** The vast majority of the market falls into the "Mid-range" and "Budget" price segments. True "Luxury" listings are rare, meaning hosts in that segment face less direct competition but must maintain exceptional ratings.
4. **Seasonal Price Fluctuations:** Average prices tend to peak during certain months (often summer or local peak seasons) while availability drops. This indicates a clear seasonal spike in demand where hosts can safely increase their rates.
5. **Pricing Strategy Inefficiencies:** A surprising percentage of listings are flagged as "Underpriced" compared to their city average. This indicates that many hosts could likely increase their daily rates without significantly hurting their booking volume, leaving money on the table.
6. **Ratings vs. Price Disconnect:** The highest-priced listings do not consistently guarantee a perfect 5-star rating. Guests factor in the overall experience and value for money, showing that excellent hospitality and cleanliness can allow budget stays to compete with luxury amenities.
